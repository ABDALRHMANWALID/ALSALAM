Law regulating the use of financial technology in non-banking financial activities

Dr. Mohamed Omran, Chairman of the Financial Supervisory Authority, welcomed the issuance by the President of the Republic of Law No. (5) of 2022 on “Regulating and Developing the Use of Financial Technology in Non-Banking Financial Activities” and its publication in the Official Gazette No. 5 bis, and stressed that the Authority’s Board of Directors is keen to speed up Issuing the necessary executive decisions to put into effect the provisions of the articles of the law regulating the use of financial technology in non-banking financial activities. Management of the authority in implementation of the provisions of the articles of the law. He also expressed his appreciation to the members of the House of Representatives and their contributions during the consideration and study of the draft law throughout the discussion sessions.

The Chairman of the Authority said that the law regulating the use of financial technology in non-banking financial activities represents one of the steps of the Authority's ambitious program to develop the non-banking financial sector, as it facilitates access to the largest possible base of customers wishing to benefit from non-bank financial activities, raise their efficiency, and reduce costs. necessary to benefit from these activities and services, in addition to the qualitative leap that it will achieve by providing a license - in principle - to practice 4Electronic applications, foremost of which are electronic applications for financial advisor programs, which analyze clients’ data, their current financial status and future financial goals to provide them with technical advice, in addition to electronic applications for microfinance, electronic applications for insurance, and consumer financing after the authority monitored the accelerating global trend and the competition of countries among themselves in hiring The technology of these applications in the non-banking financial sector and what is known as “FinTech”.

He added that the eighth article of the law has authorized the authority’s board of directors to adopt other electronic applications as long as they have the authority to perform the tasks of non-banking financial activities, and have systems to protect the data of customers from electronic intrusion and cyber attacks, and comply with the authority’s controls on verifying digital identity and digital contracts in Practicing non-banking financial activities and anti-money laundering controls.

Dr. Omran revealed that the practitioners of non-bank financial activities using digital financial technology will be divided into two categories; The first represents companies wishing to engage in non-bank financial activities through digital financial technology technologies. These companies will fully conduct their activities using digital technology, after they are established and licensed by the Authority in accordance with the provisions of the law.

He added that the second category represents companies and entities that are currently licensed by the authority to engage in any non-banking financial activities, and they wish to engage in these activities through the use of some areas of financial technology. The use of modern technological means, either by itself or through a third party among those registered in a register prepared for this purpose - in the Authority to record the outsourcing bodies - to undertake some tasks or activities on their behalf, provided that this is in accordance with an outsourcing agreement concluded between them in this regard.

Dr. explained. Omran stated that the law “regulating and developing the use of financial technology in non-banking financial activities” included in its second articles that the Financial Supervisory Authority is the only administrative authority competent to apply the provisions of the law; In order to achieve the objectives of the law, it may take the procedures for establishing companies and granting the necessary licenses and approvals to practice the activities stipulated in this law, in addition to the authority’s supervising and controlling companies and entities subject to the provisions of the law regulating and developing the use of financial technology in non-banking financial activities, and receiving complaints submitted by those with The matter of those dealing with these companies if they violate the provisions or decisions issued, with the authority's obligation to respond to these complaints within a period not exceeding one month from the date of completing the documents specified by the authority.

The head of the authority also indicated that the provisions of the articles of the law “regulating and developing the use of financial technology in non-banking financial activities” have made it possible for the authority to do so by itself or in partnership with others, or to entrust this to one of the competent authorities to establish a regulatory laboratory for applications that allows practitioners of non-banking financial activities to use technology Under the supervision and control of the Authority, or for those wishing to be registered or registered in the registry of providing outsourcing services to test innovative financial technology applications, including business models and related mechanisms, on real clients in preparation for their submission to clients.

It gave the Authority the issuance of a temporary license for financial technology startups (FinTech Startup License) for a period not exceeding two years, to support innovative startup companies in the field of financial technology in their submission of non-banking financial activities and products, in accordance with the terms, controls and licensing procedures set by the Authority’s Board of Directors, and the minimum Its issued capital shall be no less than two hundred and fifty thousand pounds, and start-up companies are also exempted from licensing fees.

Dr. noted.Omran stated that, on the other hand, the law will facilitate the authority’s performance of its supervisory role over the entities subject to it with regard to compliance with standards of transparency and governance, and the protection of dealers in non-bank financial markets by using modern and innovative technological applications while exercising its supervisory role (SupTech), in addition to using artificial intelligence mechanisms and others. One of the digital models for detecting facts that constitute violations of the laws regulating non-bank financial activities, and suspicion of money laundering, in addition to early warning of risks related to liquidity, financing or other matters related to financial stability, in a way that reflects the Egyptian state’s adoption of a policy of providing an efficient work environment. Remotely based mainly on the secure exchange of information and the supervision of a censor oriented with the same ability towards digital control, especially in light of the generous spending on information technology in the Middle East and North Africa, which is expected to reach $200 billion inThis year, according to the latest forecasts issued by consulting and research institutions.

It is worth noting that the trend towards employing financial technology in the non-banking financial sector and the necessary supervisory role that depends on technology, had its harbingers during the past four years after conducting successful realistic experiences in employing technology in operations within non-bank financial services, such as allowing the electronic issuance of four insurance coverages. Typical- characterized by a large number of dealers, such as compulsory insurance for civil liability arising from driving rapid transit vehicles, as 14 insurance companies were allowed to issue compulsory cars electronically before the car insurance group was established, and the authority issued to 16 insurance companies approving the license to issue Travel insurance coverages electronically, and 5 insurance companies that were licensed to issue a personal accident document electronically - before the establishment of the travel insurance group, which took over the provision of insurance coverage for Egyptians traveling abroad and issued its documents electronically in mid-2021.

There are also nearly 15,000 ordinary citizens benefiting from the microfinance product known as nanofinance by facilitating granting financing through financial portfolios on their mobile phones, with financing balances amounting to 3.7 million pounds by the end of 2021.
